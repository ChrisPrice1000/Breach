Roblox data brench: What you need to know


Chris Price • Data Breach

September 20, 2022

Valued at $38 billion and boasting over 200 million monthly users, Roblox is the world's most popular online gaming platform, with a core demographic of children aged between 9 to 15.


Despite its commercial success and 17-year history of development, intruders were able to use an application programming interface (API) to obtain millions of user records. This includes names, passwords, email addresses and other identifiable records.


An API is a go-between for two different pieces of software. A popular example is weather APIs; most weather apps get condition information from an API belonging to an organisation like the Bureau of Meterology, which actually physically collects the data.


In this case, it's believed that the people behind this data breach, were able to access roblox API that did not require authentication to access user information. The suspected API endpoint is now offline, meaning that no further information will be exposed.


What should roblox users do now?

Roblox is still encouraging, it's users to be "extra vigilant".

But what does Roblox means by this?

• Look out for any suspicious or unexpected activity across your online accounts.
• Look out for suspicious emails, texts, phone calls or messages on social media.
• Never click on any links that look suspicious or provide passwords, personal or financial information.

Scamwatchs has advised roblox users to secure their personal information by changing online account passwords and enabling multi-factor authentication on their accounts.


Who is at risk from the roblox data brench?

We don't yet know for sure. Mostly likely accounts that has valuable items, that can be sold for in-game currency.

What we do know is that this was a sophisticated attack. The ip address kept moving and came out of various countries in europe. The FBI is working with roblox to obtain information and evidence needed to conduct the criminal investigation. Roblox saids it took action to block the attack as soon as it learned of the brench and that "not everyone may be affected.
